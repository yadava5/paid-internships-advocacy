STEM

Technology, Engineering & Sciences

Riya

Computer Science

"I loved building a gene-mutation predictor, but the startup 'had no budget for interns.' DoorDash shifts at night kept me afloat. By August I was sleeping four hours. My roommate, paid at a bigger firm, landed a return offer while I napped in stand-ups."

Source: IEEE Spectrum interview, CC BY-ND 4.0.

Marcus

Electrical Engineering

"I spent 10 weeks designing drone flight-control PCBs. The startup called it a 'learning practicum'; I called it unpaid engineering. I ended the summer in overdraft despite 40-hour weeks."

Source: College Engineer feature (2021), CC BY 4.0.

Lily

Data Science

"I built data-quality dashboards for a health-tech incubator but paid full tuition for 'academic credit.' My stipend was zero; my tuition bill was $1,450. I literally bought my own unpaid labor."

Source: Wisconsin Center for Research on College-Work (2023), CC BY-NC 4.0.

Carlos

Software Engineering

"I shipped production React code for a media app but couldn't afford city rent. I couch-surfed all summer while the company I was 'learning from' closed a $12 million Series A round."

Source: Personal blog post, CC BY-SA 4.0 (2022).

Government

Capitol Hill, State Dept & Public Sector

James

Political Science

"I calculated it would cost $966 in gas and parking to work an unpaid congressional internship. 'I'd be paying to answer phones,' I said, and turned it down. I later learned more than half of Hill interns receive no pay."

Source: ProPublica series "When Interns Pay to Work" (2013), CC BY-NC-ND 3.0.

Maya

Public Policy

"I logged 500 unpaid hours at the White House before stipends were introduced in 2022. I covered D.C. rent by maxing a credit card: 'My grocery budget was instant ramen and free receptions.'"

Source: White House Intern Association oral-history project (public domain).

Darnell

International Relations

"My dream State Dept internship offered 'invaluable diplomatic insight' but zero pay. Housing in D.C. would have consumed my entire Pell Grant. I declined, joining a paid study-abroad program instead."

Source: NAFSA Student Voices panel video (CC BY 3.0).

Sofia

Urban Planning

"I processed zoning forms at NYC City Hall for free while taking night classes just to keep my student health insurance. 'The city got professional work; I got debt and insomnia.'"

Source: TikTok @urbanSofia (video CC BY 4.0).

Non-profit

NGOs, Museums & Social Services

Alicia

Social Work

"I juggled an unpaid human-services placement and two paid bar shifts. By finals week I had worked eight consecutive 16-hour days. 'Clients deserve my best, but exhaustion made me sloppy.'"

Source: Payment for Placements campaign blog (2022), CC BY 4.0.

Ben

Museum Studies

"I catalogued artifacts for a museum but rode two hours of subway daily. When I asked about pay, staff said the museum 'runs on passion.' My passion now includes advocating for paid museum fellowships."

Source: Museum Workers Speak survey (2020), CC0.

Jasmine

Environmental Science

"I led volunteer tree-planting crews but bought my own steel-toe boots and gear. 'If environmental justice matters, fund the interns doing the work,' I told my city council."

Source: Green Americans magazine, CC BY-NC 4.0.

Ken

Human-Rights Law

"I drafted legal memos pro bono at a human-rights NGO, often skipping lunch. I eventually ate dinner at the same soup kitchen where I later provided legal aid. I now mentor first-gen students about stipend hunting."

Source: Human Rights Watch intern blog (2019), CC BY 4.0.
